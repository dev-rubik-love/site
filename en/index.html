He's analyzing user behavior and producing exact math value.

The math value exactly describing on how
the mindset of one user matches with another.

The set of math values allows building the grid around the user
of what things and people he is interested in.

The set of grids allows binding a comprehensive global grid
from every user to every user, just like atoms bound in the universe ...

Now we are ready to provide an entirely new quality
of matching and people connection in the world of social networks and dating.

This is possible, and we will do it in the future, but for now, it has a few problems:

- Why we are ultimately better than all other dating services?
Other dating services able to provide result with only strictly limited quantity-quality point. We can produce result with any quantity-quality point.

- Why people wants one more dating service?
- Current sites of dating are not working. Their quantity and sameness prove this. If at least 1 of them worked, he would fill the entire niche. This is reminiscent of the abundance of forums before the coming of social networks like "facebook".

- How the algorithm works?
- We send posts that come to all users.
Users rate these posts with "like" or "dislike".
We track these ratings and find people who have given the same rating to the same post.
At first time, the accuracy is low, but already after a week, every user may have > 2^28 combinations.
It's making every user unique. With every new like, a group matches narrows down literally to a single person, and the accuracy proportionally grows to almost 100% -
- Simple enough to be lightweight and scalable, efficient enough to be first in the market.

- Why do we resemble google?
We have a similar algorithm. Google is used to search for sites, and we are searching for people.
The most basic and very first Google algorithm did the following:
Other search engines ("Yahoo", "AltaVista", etc.) returned results based on the number of target words on the site page.
Google started to show results based on the number of targeted links.
Good sites have more links leading to them. We are doing the same.
Other "Search people engines" give results by the count of target words in the user profile.
Rubik starts to give results based on the count of target posts.
Good-matching people have more matched posts that lead only to them.

- Why do we resemble asymmetric cryptography encryption?
- The heart of asymmetric encryption is the idea that we can't do something to be 100% endless in the universe, but we are doing it so long that it's 100% enough for our whole life.
We have used the same idea. The dating services paradox of X and 2X can't be solved endlessly for 100%, but we are doing that for so long that enough for the whole human life.

Our key advantages

Profile autofill.
- Filling out the profile is the most painful problem of such services, both for the users and for the platforms. Platforms need a minimal set of information about users to provide quality service, and users are not ready to spend much time on profile filling. Eventually, platforms are losing money, and users are missing quality service. We autofill the user profile on the results of the algorithm job.

Touchpoint.
In other social networks people can't start the communication comfortable
Touchpoint. In other social networks, people can't start communication comfortably because they do not know the touchpoint of each other. Social networks with uncomfortable communication miss a lot of benefits.
- Our algorithm calculates the touchpoint for a user and shows the result. Now two totally unknown users can comfortably start communication without fearing being ignored.

Safe and effective chat.
- Safe chat is an important metric for every social network. Some dating services even position safe chat as their key advantage.
In our service, it's only one of the key advantages.
Here it's safe because a user can choose a boundary of mindset matching before starting the communication.
It's effective because users can skip standard tedious questions and immediately start speaking about hobbies of each other.

Your hobbies are exact.
In a regular service, if you just list your hobbies - there will be many "misses". User 1 wrote "rollers", and user 2 "roller skates", and that is, no match will happen. User 1 wrote "ping-pong," and user 2 wrote "table tennis" - it's the same, but the match will be missed. Moreover, many films, games, and gods have different names in each country for marketing purposes, it's making standard match system useless.

Result guaranteed.
How much time do you need to spend on a dating site to achieve a guaranteed result? At least 50%, 10%, or 5% of this time? In other services, the time you spent in the past does not affect the future result in any way. In our case, the accuracy increases with each put "like". 500 likes are enough to get high-quality matches. The ultimate quality is limited only by your desire.

Helps people to research itself
- The person's mind is a mystery, even for the person himself. Exploring it is one of the biggest humanity questions. We will help users to research themselves with a set of our features. Likes provided by a user allow building a grid of his hobbies, preferences, etc. Dataanalise will reveal dependencies that, for now, are hidden from humanity.

Controlled outcome.
Do you want a soulmate that will share your mind exactly the 20% or 80% percentage?It's manageable!
Any number from 0 to 100, any count of matches and mismatches.
Each user may choose his own "perfect match" coefficient, and the service will provide the corresponding matches.

Instantaneous descriptive information about the user.
Let's imagine you met a new friend and want to know more about him.
You no more need to ask boring questions to each other about what you love or not.
Just share your profile! He will give a lot of key information. It's not only the primary information about the age, business, location and etc. It's first of all a fast information about music, films, hobbies, politic, point of view and etc.

Fast result.
This advantage may not be desirable for product evolution, but experience the power of the algorithm.
One minute - this is how much time a user needs per day to get a high-quality result.
This time is enough to open the site or application, put the "likes" and get the result whenever you want.
The algorithm will do all the stuff for you.
